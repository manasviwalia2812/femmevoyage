BEFORE YOU GO:

  • Research Your Destination: Learn about the local culture, customs, and laws regarding women's safety. Research safe neighborhoods and accommodations.
  • Share Your Itinerary: Share your travel plans, including accommodations and activities, with a trusted friend or family member. Keep them updated on any changes to your plans.
  • Travel Insurance: Purchase travel insurance that includes coverage for medical emergencies, trip cancellations, and theft.
  • Emergency Contacts: Save important phone numbers, including local emergency services, your country's embassy or consulate, and your travel insurance provider.

PACKING:

  • Security Items: Carry a money belt or hidden pouch to store your valuables, such as passport, cash, and cards. Consider bringing a doorstop alarm or portable door lock for added security in accommodations.
  • Clothing: Dress modestly and respect local customs to avoid unwanted attention. Pack clothing that allows for easy movement and is appropriate for the destination's climate and culture.
  • Personal Safety Items: Carry a whistle or personal alarm to attract attention in case of emergency. Pepper spray or a similar self-defense tool may provide added security in certain situations.
